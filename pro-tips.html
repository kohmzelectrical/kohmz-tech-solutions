Electrical Pro Tips & Safety

Advanced engineering insights for maintaining a safe, efficient, and code-compliant electrical system at home or in the office.

Advice from the KOHMZ Engineering Team

1. Panel Board Load Evaluation

Flickering lights when the AC turns on? Your panel may be undersized. A professional load evaluation ensures your breakers match your current power consumption to avoid nuisance tripping and overheating.

2. Dedicated Circuit Rule

Heavy appliances like ACs, Water Heaters, and Pumps must have their own "Dedicated Circuit." Sharing a circuit with outlets can melt wires inside the wall and cause structural fires over time.

3. Sparking Outlet Diagnostics

A sparking outlet usually indicates a loose connection or arc-fault. If you see sparks, turn off the breaker immediately. Loose wiring creates high resistance, which leads to intense heat and electrical combustion.

4. Insulation Resistance (Megger Test)

Hidden micro-shorts are invisible. Before final wiring, KOHMZ uses a Megohmmeter to test wire insulation. This ensures that pests or heat haven't compromised the integrity of the wires inside your conduits.

5. GFCI in Wet Areas

Outlets near sinks, bathrooms, or outdoors MUST be GFCI-protected (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter). This shuts off power in milliseconds if water contact is detected, preventing fatal electric shocks.

6. Surge Protective Devices (SPD)

Voltage spikes from the grid or lightning can fry expensive appliances (Inverters, Smart TVs). Installing a Panel-Level SPD provides a "safety valve" that diverts excess voltage safely to the ground.

7. Proper Grounding (Earthing)

The 3rd pin on your plug is there for your life. A proper grounding system provides a path for fault current to escape. Without it, your metal appliance bodies could become "live" and shock anyone who touches them.

8. Extension Cord Safety

Extension cords are temporary solutions, not permanent wiring. Daisy-chaining (connecting one strip to another) creates a massive fire hazard. If you need more outlets, it's time to install new wall receptacles.

9. LED Retrofitting Benefits

Switching to LEDs isn't just about the bill. LEDs run much cooler than halogen/incandescent bulbs. Reducing heat in your lighting fixtures extends the lifespan of the wire insulation above your ceiling.

10. CAD Electrical Drawings

Never start a renovation without an Electrical Plan. CAD drawings and Load Schedules act as your system's blueprint, making future troubleshooting and expansions faster, cheaper, and safer.
