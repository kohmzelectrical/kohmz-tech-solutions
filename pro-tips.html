ELECTRICAL PRO TIPS & SAFETY

Learn directly from the experts. Practical advice to maintain safety and efficiency in your electrical systems.

Advice from the KOHMZ Engineering Team

1. How to Know If You Need a Panel Upgrade

If you live in an older home and experience frequently tripping circuit breakers, flickering lights when an appliance turns on, or if your panel box feels warm to the touch, your system is likely overloaded. A load evaluation and panel upgrade are required immediately to prevent electrical fires.

2. The Rule for Dedicated Circuits

Never plug heavy-duty appliances (like Air Conditioners, Water Heaters, or Microwaves) into standard room outlets or extension cords. These require a "Dedicated Circuit" — meaning they have their own specific wire running directly to their own circuit breaker in the panel. This prevents wire melting and system overloads.

3. What to Do with a Sparking Outlet

If an outlet sparks, pops, or smells like burning plastic, stop using it immediately. Go to your main panel board and turn off the breaker connected to that room. Do not attempt to tape it or fix it yourself. Call a licensed electrical contractor to perform diagnostics.

4. The Importance of Insulation Resistance Testing

A wire might look fine on the outside, but its internal insulation could be compromised due to heat, pests, or age. The "KOHMZ Standard" involves using a Digital Megohmmeter to test wire insulation before finalizing a project. This ensures there are no hidden micro-shorts inside your walls.
