Technical Advisory (FAQ)

Expert answers to the most critical electrical concerns for property owners and facility managers.

Is my property at risk? When should I upgrade my electrical panel?

Tripping breakers, flickering lights, a slight burning smell, or warm panel covers are critical red flags indicating an overloaded system. We perform comprehensive load evaluations and Digital Insulation Resistance Testing to detect internal faults before they escalate into structural fire hazards.

Do you handle dedicated technical wiring for new heavy AC units?

Yes. Simply tapping a new high-HP air conditioner into an existing line is dangerous. We provide certified AC load centers, dedicated home-run wiring, and exact breaker sizing to ensure your cooling system operates safely without stressing your existing infrastructure.

What should I do if an outlet stops working or emits sparks?

Stop using the outlet immediately and turn off the corresponding breaker at your main panel. Sparking indicates loose connections or failing insulation. Our team provides rapid technical diagnostics to isolate the faulty wiring and replace the compromised materials immediately.

Can you design the electrical layout for a new commercial space?

Absolutely. We specialize in custom wiring architecture, CAD-driven electrical designs, load scheduling, and NEMA-compliant enclosures. Our documents are meticulously prepared to pass rigorous LGU safety permits and commercial building compliance inspections without delay.

How can regular technical maintenance benefit my business?

Proactive maintenance is exponentially cheaper than disaster recovery. Preventative diagnostics avoid unexpected operational downtime, protect sensitive corporate servers from power surges, and prevent catastrophic equipment failures through systematic thermal scanning and insulation testing.

Can you install heavy-duty industrial power systems?

Yes, we are fully equipped to implement heavy entrance wiring, 3-phase service meter bases, motor controls, and commercial-grade distribution panels specifically engineered for high-capacity industrial, manufacturing, and retail electrical loads.
